Generative Crystals

Intending to get into Generative Art and p5.js, I followed the main ideas of a tutorial to generate symmetrical crystal shapes. Additionally, it gave me the possibility to further my object-oriented programming knowledge. Each crystal is defined by a different background shape (Hexagon, Circle, or Repeated Hexagons) and different ornaments (like Line Segments, Circles, or Dotted Lines). Every piece's color is randomly drawn from a given color palette. Due to the inherent randomness of the system, every new crystal will exhibit different combinations while always keeping several optical proportions.

For me, it was especially interesting to see how relatively simple code formed complex crystals. Coming from an academic and scientific coding background, it was at the same time pleasing to see such beautiful and symmetric crystals forming that easily, and weird experience, as generative and creative coding (even though having similar code syntax and structure) follow a completely different workflow. In my - admittedly very small - experience, creative coding needed much less conceptual structure and therefore I often felt like I would miss important steps of setting up the codebase. Also, the higher importance of trial-and-error made me feel incredibly clumsy at times.
