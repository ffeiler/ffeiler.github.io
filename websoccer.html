Understanding The Websoccer¶

When examining the influence tactics have on the game simulation, it is necessary to understand how game simulation really works.
There are 3 types of actions that can happen: a pass, a shot, or a tackle.
On top of that is a decider - a Football God - who determines the next action in the game.

So let us look into how the Football God decides which action will take place next.

The Football God¶

Firstly, if your Goalkeeper has the ball, the only thing he can do is to pass it to one of his teammates.

But what about the field players?

Tackle¶

The probability of a tackle depends on the formation of both teams. Let us say the player who is currently holding the ball is a midfielder. Then the probability of getting tackled depends on how many midfielders the opposition has.

Code alert

$p_{tackle} = \begin{cases} 20 + 20 (N_{opponent}^{midfield} - N_{ballholding}^{midfield}) & \text{, other team has more midfielders} \\ 40 & \text{, else} \\ \end{cases}$

\Code alert

So basically the probability of a tackle is most of the time $40\%$, but if the other team has 2 more players in the respective part of the field, it is $60\%$, if they have 3 more, it is $80\%$.

Shooting¶

The probability of getting a shot depends on three variables: the position of the ball-holding player, the tactics, and the current result. Furthermore, the probability of getting a shot can not be higher than $50\%$.

Let us disentangle this: Which influence have these three variables?

Current Result¶

This is really straightforward: If you have less goals than the other team, your team is $10\%$ more likely to shoot.

Position of the Shooter¶

Defenders have a $5\%$ chance of shooting, Midfielders $20\%$ and Strikers $35\%$.
BUT: if you play with counter-attacks, these values are halved.

Tactics¶

This depends only on your offensive strength and the opponent's defensive strength and is simply calculated: Off_strength(Ball-holding) - Def_strength (opponent).

That is the point where it stops being simple.
Because.. what the heck is offensive and defensive strength? We will take a closer look on that soon.

Passing¶

If none of the other two actions happen, players simply pass the ball. If long passes are activated, the passing attempt fails $30\%$ more often. NOTE: As there is a bug in the underlying code, long passes have NO positive effect.
If counter attacks are activated, there is a change of Offensive_Setting$\%$ to get a shot after the opposition striker's passing attempt failed.

So okay, it got a little bit more clear what is going on in the Football God's mind. But we still have to understand at least one thing: What the heck are offensive and defensive strength?

Offensive and Defensive Strength¶

... it is complicated. If you want to look at the code, you will understand the exact definition easily. But for all the others, this explanation needs to be sufficient:

Offensive Strength is the strength of your midfielders and strikers
Defensive Strength is the strength of your midfielders and defenders

$\Rightarrow$ having a strong midfield will yield success, as it is strengthens both your offense and defense

In the following, I wrote a small testing program to evaluate the strength of different possible tactics.
NOTE: For sake of simplicity, all players have strength 100.

'''
Definitions of Offensive and Defensive Strength
 - formation is given as list - example: [4,1,3,1,1]
 - off: offensive setting
'''

def offensive_strength(formation,off):
    off_fac = (80+off*0.4)/100
    mf_st = formation["DM"]*0.7*strength + formation["MF"]*strength + formation["OM"]*1.3*strength
    st_st = 0
    if formation["ST"] < 3:
        st_st = formation["ST"]*1.5*strength
    elif formation["ST"] == 3:
        st_st = 2*1.5*strength + 0.5*strength
    elif formation["ST"] == 4:
        st_st = 2*1.5*strength + 2*0.5*strength
    off_st = off_fac*(mf_st+st_st)
    return int(off_st)

def defensive_strength(formation,off):
    def_fac = (130-off*0.5)/100
    mf_st = formation["DM"]*1.3*strength + formation["MF"]*strength + formation["OM"]*0.7*strength
    df_st = 0
    if formation["DF"] < 3:
        df_st = 0.5*formation["DF"]*strength
    elif formation["DF"] > 4:
        df_st = 1.5*formation["DF"]*strength
    else:
        df_st = formation["DF"]*strength
    def_st = def_fac*(mf_st+df_st)
    return int(def_st)
